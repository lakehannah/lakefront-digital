# Sales Representative Interview Questions

## Phone Screen (15 minutes)

**Purpose:** Filter out obvious mismatches, gauge interest

1. **Tell me about your sales experience.**
   - What have you sold? B2B or B2C?
   - How did you find leads?
   - What was your quota/performance?

2. **Why sales?**
   - Why do you want to sell for Lakefront Digital specifically?
   - What motivates you — money, competition, helping people?

3. **Comfort with cold outreach?**
   - On a scale of 1-10, how comfortable are you cold calling?
   - Walk me through a cold call opening.

4. **Logistics:**
   - Are you local to Utah County?
   - Can you start immediately?
   - Commission-only vs base+commission preference?

**Green flags:**
- Has done cold outreach before
- Asks questions about the product
- Explains sales process clearly
- Seems hungry, not just looking for a job

**Red flags:**
- Only B2C retail experience, no prospecting
- Uncomfortable with commission structure
- Wants to "learn sales" (needs experience first)
- Can't explain how they'd find leads

## In-Person Interview (45 minutes)

### Section 1: Sales Skills (15 min)

**5. Role-play: I'm a plumber. You cold called me. Pitch me a website.**

**Listen for:**
- Do they ask discovery questions first? ("Do you have a website?")
- Do they focus on pain points? ("Are you getting found on Google?")
- Do they have a clear offer? ($2,500 website, done in 5 days)
- Do they ask for the sale? ("Can we schedule a demo?")

**6. Tell me about a time you lost a sale. What happened?**

**Listen for:**
- Takes accountability vs blames prospect
- Explains what they learned
- Shows resilience

**7. How do you handle rejection?**

**Listen for:**
- Realistic answer (it sucks, but you move on)
- Has systems/processes ("I do 10 more calls")
- Doesn't pretend rejection doesn't bother them

---

### Section 2: Product Understanding (10 min)

**8. Explain our product to me like I'm a salon owner.**

**Listen for:**
- Simplifies technical concepts
- Focuses on benefits (more customers, online booking)
- Mentions speed (days, not months)
- Mentions AI/automation differentiation

**9. What questions would you ask a prospect to qualify them?**

**Good answers:**
- "Do you have a website now?"
- "How do customers find you?"
- "What's your biggest challenge getting new customers?"
- "Who makes decisions about marketing?"

**10. How would you handle: "I had a website built before and it was a waste of money."**

**Good answers:**
- Acknowledges pain ("That sucks")
- Differentiates us ("Most agencies take 3 months, we take 3 days")
- Offers proof ("See our portfolio")
- Lowers risk ("We can start with a simple site")

---

### Section 3: Motivation & Fit (10 min)

**11. What does your perfect workday look like?**

**Listen for:**
- Self-directed (doesn't need hand-holding)
- Activity-focused (calls, meetings, outreach)
- Results-oriented (closes, not just "busy")

**12. Where do you see yourself in 2 years?**

**Listen for:**
- Wants to grow (Sales Lead, management)
- Committed to sales long-term
- Has ambition ("I want to make $150k+")

**13. Why should we hire you over the other candidates?**

**Listen for:**
- Specific strengths ("I have a network of contractors")
- Hunger ("I need to prove myself")
- Differentiator ("I already know the market")

---

### Section 4: Practical Questions (10 min)

**14. How would you find 20 leads this week?**

**Good answers:**
- Drive around, write down businesses with no websites
- Google Maps search for "plumbers Spanish Fork"
- Facebook groups ("Anyone know a good handyman?")
- Chamber of Commerce directory
- Referrals from existing customers

**15. What's your process for tracking leads and follow-ups?**

**Listen for:**
- Has a system (CRM, spreadsheet, notes)
- Follows up consistently
- Doesn't rely on memory

**16. Questions for us?**

**Good questions:**
- "What's the average time to close a deal?"
- "Who are your best customers?"
- "What support do you provide new sales reps?"
- "What's your biggest challenge right now?"

**Red flag questions:**
- "How quickly can I move to a different role?"
- "Can I work remotely from another state?"
- "Do I have to cold call?"

---

## Scoring

Rate each section 1-5:
- **1-2:** Poor fit, don't hire
- **3:** Maybe, but has gaps
- **4:** Good fit, some concerns
- **5:** Excellent, hire immediately

**Sections:**
- Sales Skills (questions 5-7)
- Product Understanding (questions 8-10)
- Motivation & Fit (questions 11-13)
- Practical Skills (questions 14-15)

**Total score:**
- **16-20:** Strong hire
- **12-15:** Hire with reservations
- **Below 12:** Don't hire

## Reference Check Questions

**If you call their previous sales manager:**

1. "How would you rate their prospecting skills?"
2. "Did they hit quota consistently?"
3. "How did they handle rejection?"
4. "Would you hire them again?"
5. "What was their biggest weakness?"

**Red flags:**
- "They were okay but not great"
- "Struggled with cold outreach"
- "Lots of activity, few closes"
- "Wouldn't rehire"
